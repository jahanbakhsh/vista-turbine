INTRODUCTION

VISTA’s dedicated engine shop performs all levels of on- and off-wing aircraft engine work in wide range of maintenance activities from borescope inspections to full engine overhaul. VISTA obtained the PART-145 certification for a maintenance organization from the Civil Aviation Organisation of Iran. We offer top quality services to our customers. Our newly engine shop is staffed by highly skilled engine technicians and engineers who provide major repairs on Pratt & Whitney, CFM International and IAE engines including full major periodic inspection (MPI). We also perform line maintenance found on the aircraft we maintain. VISTA ensures that our customer’s engines have been serviced according to OEM manuals and recommendations.

Engineering Services

a) ECTM

Modern engine maintenance approach is combination of on-condition and also condition monitoring concept. You can wait for a failure (on condition) or you can predict a failure (condition monitoring). Engine Condition Trend Monitoring (ECTM) is a technique to continuously monitor the health of engines in order to find symptoms of damage, deterioration or excessive wear. By monitoring a known set of parameters, usually altitude, outside air temperature (OAT), air speed, ITT, N1/N2 RPMs, fuel flow and vibrations, it’s possible to predict needed maintenance before a failure occurs. VISTA has developed engine monitoring and diagnostics services, based on the analysis of technical and flight data, along with recommendations for fleet maintenance or management. Our ECTM services including generation of warnings, customer notifications and recommended maintenance actions. As part of our Fleet management services, VISTA regularly receives flight data from the operator in real time. We analyze this data and, if needed, propose corrective actions. There is possibility for the operators to perform remote borescope inspection, so that local borescopers at airlines can consult with VISTA engine experts in the least possible time. As a result the engine life and mean time between shop visit will be increased, maintenance overall cost will be decreased and finally the main domain of aircraft maintenance would be efficiently managed.

b) Asset management

Engines are a vital part of overall aircraft value: they represent 30% of the value of a new aircraft, and 80% after 15 years. Conserving this residual or resale value is a primary goal of the air transport industry, especially low-cost carriers and leasing firms. VISTA Asset Management department have access to the comprehensive toolbox of processes and experienced staff which are essential to providing lessors with effective asset management services. This ensures our clients receive balanced information, evaluation data and advice. We can monitor the technical condition of a range of engines during the term of their lease, and periodically review the agreed schedule of inspections and maintenance reserves to adjust for variations in the technical or financial performance of the lessee. VISTA can provide teams of Engine specialists in both documentary and physical survey fields to inspect engine during the lease term at the operator’s facility or convenient airport locations and audit the engine's maintenance records as required. We can provide oversight that will identify any shortcomings in the management of the asset, and provide advice as to the most suitable way to address those discrepancies. By co-operating with lessees VISTA can ensure that all the requirements of the lease contracts are being strictly complied with, providing better value in managing the aircraft lease. Finally, VISTA can provide expertise in maintenance reserve analysis ensuring that any reserves paid by the lessee are appropriately distributed in order to, preserve the full residual value of the asset.

Taban Air is an airline headquartered in the Ekbatan Complex in Tehran, Iran with its main operational base in Mashhad. It operates international, domestic and charter routes as a scheduled carrier.

Iran Air

Iran Air, branded as The Airline of the Islamic Republic of Iran , is the flag carrier of Iran headquartered on the grounds of Mehrabad Airport in Tehran. As of 2018, it operates scheduled services to 71 destinations in Asia and Europe. Iran Air’s main bases are Imam Khomeini International Airport and Mehrabad Airport, both situated in Tehran, capital of Iran. Domestically, Iran Air is commonly known as Homa, which is the name of a mythical Persian griffin, and also the acronym of Iran National Airlines in the Persian language.
